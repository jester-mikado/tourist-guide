Thanjavur

Thanjavur District, located in Tamil Nadu, India, is renowned for its rich cultural heritage and historical significance. Often referred to as the "Rice Bowl of Tamil Nadu," the district is known for its agricultural wealth, especially in paddy cultivation.

Brihadeeswarar Temple

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this grand temple is a masterpiece of Chola architecture, renowned for its massive granite dome and intricate sculptures.

TIMING: 6:00 AM - 12:30 PM, 4:00 PM - 8:30 PM
Close: Special festivals and events might affect timings.

Thanjavur Palace

This palace complex includes the Raja Rajan Museum, Art Gallery, and the Saraswathi Mahal Library. It showcases a mix of Dravidian and Islamic architecture.

TIMING: 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM
Close: Friday's

Airavatesvara Temple

Located in Darasuram, this temple is another UNESCO World Heritage site renowned for its intricate carvings and architectural brilliance.

TIMING:: 6:00 AM - 12:30 PM, 4:00 PM - 8:00 PM

Swamimalai

Known for the Swaminathaswamy Temple, Swamimalai is a prominent pilgrimage site dedicated to Lord Murugan.

TIMING : 6:00 AM - 12:00 PM, 4:00 PM - 8:00 PM

Poovarasankuppam

This village is known for its traditional pottery and arts, offering a glimpse into local crafts and culture.

TIMING:Typically visits are scheduled by appointment.

Tanjore Painting Art Gallery

Located in the Tanjore Palace, this gallery showcases the traditional Tanjore paintings known for their rich colors and intricate details.

TIMING : 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM

Maratha Palace

Part of the Thanjavur Palace, this building reflects the Maratha style of architecture and includes a museum with various artifacts.

9:00 AM - 5:00 PM

Kollidam River

This river, a tributary of the Cauvery, flows through the Thanjavur district and is known for its scenic beauty and tranquility. It’s a good spot for nature lovers and those looking to relax by the water.

TIMING:Open throughout the day, but early mornings or late afternoons are preferable for a peaceful experience.






Foods To Try


1.Thanjavur Briyani

A flavorful rice dish cooked with aromatic spices, meat (usually chicken or mutton), and sometimes vegetables. It's distinct from other biryanis in its preparation and seasoning, often featuring local spices and herbs.

2.Thanjavur Special Dosa

A variant of the traditional dosa, this version is often larger, crispier, and might be served with unique chutneys or fillings that are specific to the region.

3.Manga Pachadi

A tangy and sweet dish made with raw mango, jaggery, and spices. It's a traditional dish typically prepared during festivals and special occasions.

Where to Try: Local homes and traditional eateries during festive seasons.

4.Thanjavur Paruppu

A special type of dal cooked with local spices, often served with rice. It is known for its unique blend of flavors and textures.

Where to Try: Traditional South Indian restaurants and homes.

5.Thanjavur Jaggery

Known for its high quality, Thanjavur jaggery is made from sugarcane and has a distinct taste and texture. It is used in various sweets and dishes.

Where to Try: Local markets and sweet shops.

6.Pongal

A savory dish made with rice and moong dal, seasoned with black pepper, ginger, and ghee. It's often garnished with cashews and curry leaves. While Pongal is common throughout South India, the Thanjavur variant has its own local twist.

Where to Try: Breakfast spots and traditional South Indian restaurants.

7.Kuzhi Paniyaram

Small, round savory dumplings made from fermented rice and urad dal batter, often seasoned with onions, curry leaves, and spices. They are crispy on the outside and soft on the inside.

Where to Try: Street food vendors and traditional South Indian restaurants.

8.Halwa

About: A sweet made from wheat, sugar, ghee, and flavored with cardamom. It is known for its rich taste and smooth texture.

Where to Try: Specialty sweet shops in Thanjavur.
