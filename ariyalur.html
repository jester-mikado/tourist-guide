Ariyalur

Ariyalur district, located in the Indian state of Tamil Nadu, is known for its rich cultural heritage and significant limestone deposits. It's renowned for its ancient temples, including those with Chola architectural influences, and its vibrant agricultural landscape. The district plays a key role in the region's cement industry due to its extensive limestone reserves.

Foods To Try


1.Karamani Sundal

This is a savory snack made with black-eyed peas cooked with mustard seeds, curry leaves, and grated coconut. It's a traditional snack often enjoyed during festivals and special occasions in Ariyalur.

2. Gingelly Oil-Based Dishes

Gingelly oil is a staple in Tamil Nadu cuisine, and in Ariyalur, it is used extensively in preparing various dishes. You might find unique preparations of sambar or poriyal (stir-fried vegetables) that use this oil, adding a distinctive flavor.

3.Traditional Pickles

Homemade pickles made with locally grown vegetables and fruits are quite popular. These pickles are often prepared with a variety of spices and might include regional ingredients unique to the area.

4.Kollu Dishes

Ariyalur, like many parts of Tamil Nadu, makes extensive use of kollu (horsegram) in various preparations. Dishes like kollu chutney or kollu paruppu (dal) are local favorites.
