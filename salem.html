Salem

Salem is a city in Massachusetts, famously known for its historic witch trials of 1692, which cast a long shadow over its legacy. Today, it embraces its past with numerous museums and tours dedicated to the events. Beyond its dark history, Salem is a vibrant coastal city with charming streets, a rich maritime history, and a thriving arts scene.

Often called the “Queen of Hill Stations,” Yercaud is known for its pleasant climate, coffee plantations, and beautiful viewpoints.

Ayodhyapattinam

This area is known for the ancient Kottai Perumal Temple, which showcases intricate Dravidian architecture and is a significant pilgrimage site

Mettur Dam

A major multipurpose dam on the Kaveri River, it’s an impressive engineering feat and offers scenic views of the reservoir and surrounding landscapes.

Timing : 9 AM to 6 PM.

Hogenakkal Falls

Often referred to as the “Niagara of India,” Hogenakkal Falls is a stunning waterfall on the Kaveri River. It is famous for its unique medicinal properties and the boat rides available in the area.

TIMING: 8 AM to 6 PM






Foods To Try


1.Salem Mangoes

Salem is renowned for its high-quality mangoes, especially the Salem Bangi variety. These mangoes are known for their sweetness and unique flavor, making them a local delicacy. They are often enjoyed fresh or used in various traditional dishes and desserts.

2.Kollu Rasam

This is a spicy and tangy soup made with horse gram (kollu), tamarind, and a blend of aromatic spices. It's a traditional dish in the Salem region and is valued for its nutritional benefits and unique taste.

3.Idiyappam

Idiyappam, or string hoppers, is a common South Indian dish. In Salem, it is often served with a variety of accompaniments like coconut milk, curry, or sweetened coconut. The local preparation might have slight variations in spices and ingredients.

4.Salem Roti

This is a unique type of roti or flatbread that has a distinct texture and taste, often served with spicy curries or vegetables. It’s a staple in the local cuisine and can be found in many eateries in the region.

5.Special Vathal

Vathal refers to sun-dried vegetables or fruits that are used in South Indian cooking. Salem has its unique varieties, such as vathal made from tomatoes or tamarind, which are used to add a tangy flavor to curries and rice dishes.

6.Thengai Sambar

This is a traditional South Indian sambar with a unique twist from Salem, incorporating freshly grated coconut (thengai) for a richer flavor. It is typically enjoyed with rice or idli and is a staple in local cuisine.

7. Fried Rice

Known for its distinctive taste, Salem’s fried rice often includes local ingredients like freshly ground spices and regional vegetables. It might have a unique preparation style that sets it apart from other versions found in South India.

8.Kothu Parotta

A popular street food in Salem, Kothu Parotta is made from shredded parottas stir-fried with spices, vegetables, and sometimes meat. It’s a flavorful and hearty dish that’s beloved by locals.
