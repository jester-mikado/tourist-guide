Dindigul

Dindigul district is located in the southern part of Tamil Nadu, India. Known for its picturesque landscapes, it is famous for the Dindigul Fort, a historic fortress with panoramic views of the surrounding area. The district is also renowned for its production of quality locks and the delicious Dindigul biryani, a flavorful rice dish.

Dindigul Fort

A historic fort with impressive architecture and panoramic views of the surrounding area. Built by the Nayak kings, it has a rich history.

TIMING:9:00 AM to 6:00 PM.

Kodaikanal

A well-known hill station near Dindigul, offering attractions such as Kodai Lake, Coaker’s Walk, and Bryant Park.

TIMING:9:00 AM to 6:00 PM.

Bodinayakkanur

A scenic town known for its tea estates and lush greenery. It's a good spot for nature lovers and those interested in local tea production.

TIMING::Accessible throughout the day; specific tea estates may have their own timings.

Sirumalai Hills

An offbeat hill station with trekking opportunities and beautiful landscapes, ideal for nature enthusiasts looking for tranquility.

TIMING : Open throughout the day; trekking is best done during daylight hours.

Perumal Peak

A popular trekking destination in the Western Ghats, offering breathtaking views and a challenging hike.

TIMING:Treks usually start early in the morning; plan to finish by late afternoon.

Saptur

A place with ancient temples and cultural heritage. It’s known for its historical significance and traditional architecture.

TIMING : 6:00 AM to 12:00 PM and 4:00 PM to 8:00 PM.

Vathalagundu

A town known for its coffee and spice plantations. It’s a great place to explore local agriculture and enjoy the rural landscape.

Timing :Accessible throughout the day; local farms may have specific visiting hours.

Meghamalai

Known as the “High Wavy Mountains,” it’s an untouched paradise with tea estates, wildlife, and stunning views.

TIMING: Best visited during daylight hours; some areas might require prior permission.

Shola National Park

A protected area offering rich biodiversity, including various species of flora and fauna. It’s ideal for nature walks and wildlife observation.

TIMING: 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM

Chinnamanur

A town with historical temples and local culture. It offers insight into traditional Tamil Nadu life and architecture.

TIMING :6:00 AM to 12:00 PM and 4:00 PM to 8:00 PM.






Foods To Try


1.Dindigul Thalappakatti Biryani

An iconic variant of Dindigul Biryani, this dish is named after the famous Thalappakatti Hotel in Dindigul. It's known for its unique preparation style, which involves slow-cooking the biryani with a blend of spices and herbs that give it a distinct taste.

2. Kari Dosa

A variation of the traditional dosa, Kari Dosa is served with spicy meat curry. The dosa itself is crispy and thin, while the curry is rich and spicy, often including mutton or chicken.

3.Mutton Chukka

A spicy, dry-fried mutton dish that is highly flavorful. It's typically made with tender pieces of mutton cooked with a blend of spices, onions, and curry leaves..

4.Kola Urundai

Spicy, deep-fried meatballs made from minced meat (usually beef or mutton), mixed with spices and herbs. It's a popular snack or side dish.

5.Chicken Fry

A flavorful chicken dish marinated in a blend of spices and then fried until crispy. It's known for its distinctive spicy taste.

6.Idiyappam with Curry

Idiyappam, also known as string hoppers, is a steamed dish made from rice flour, typically served with various types of curries, such as coconut milk-based or spicy meat curries.

7.Chettinad Parotta

A flaky, layered bread that’s often served with spicy gravies or curries. Chettinad Parotta is a staple in Dindigul and is known for its unique texture and taste.

8.Pulao with Raita

A rice dish cooked with a variety of vegetables or meat, often served with a refreshing yogurt-based raita. This pulao is distinct to the region and carries the local flavors of Dindigul.
