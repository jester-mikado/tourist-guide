Villupuram

Villupuram is a city located in the state of Tamil Nadu, India. It serves as the administrative headquarters of the Villupuram district. Known for its historical and cultural significance, Villupuram is situated approximately 160 kilometers southwest of Chennai. The city has a rich heritage, with several ancient temples and historic sites in the vicinity

Kailasanathar Temple

Situated in Villupuram, this ancient temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is known for its Dravidian architectural style, with intricate carvings and sculptural work that reflect the artistic skills of the era.

TIMING:6:00 AM to 12:00 PM and 4:00 PM to 8:00 PM

Sri Kaliswari Temple

Also located in Villupuram, this temple is dedicated to Goddess Kali. It is an important site for local devotees and features regular rituals and festivals that draw both locals and visitors.

TIMING:6:00 AM to 12:00 PM and 4:00 PM to 8:00 PM

Government Museum

This museum houses a collection of artifacts, sculptures, and historical items from the region, providing insight into the local history and culture.

TIMING: 10:00 AM to 5:00 PM
Close:Monday

Muthumari Amman Temple

Located in the town, this temple is dedicated to the goddess Muthumari. It is renowned for its vibrant festivals and religious significance in the local community.

TIMING :6:00 AM to 12:00 PM and 4:00 PM to 8:00 PM

Jadayu Rock

Situated on the outskirts of Villupuram, this rock formation is associated with the legendary Jadayu from the Ramayana epic. It offers a picturesque setting and a connection to local folklore.

TIMING:6:00 AM to 6:00 PM

Thiruvakkarai Fossil Wood Park

Situated around 50 kilometers from Villupuram, this park features fossilized wood dating back to the Tertiary period, offering a unique glimpse into prehistoric flora.

TIMING :9:00 AM to 5:00 PM
Close:Monday






Foods To Try


1.Paal Payasam

A traditional South Indian dessert made from rice, milk, and sugar, often flavored with cardamom and garnished with nuts.

2.Upma

A savory dish made from semolina (rava) cooked with vegetables and spices, often enjoyed as a breakfast item.

3.Mutton Chukka

A spicy, dry-fried mutton dish that is rich in flavor and typically enjoyed as an accompaniment to rice or as a standalone snack.

4.Kothu Parotta

A popular street food made by chopping up flaky parottas (layered flatbreads) and stir-frying them with a mix of vegetables, meat, and spices.

5.Pongal

A comforting dish made with rice and moong dal (lentils), cooked with black pepper, ginger, and ghee. It’s usually served with coconut chutney and sambar.

6.Kadalai Curry

A spicy chickpea curry often served with rice or parotta, featuring a rich blend of spices and tamarind.

7.Avial

A mixed vegetable dish cooked with coconut, yogurt, and a blend of spices. It’s a traditional dish often found in Kerala and Tamil Nadu cuisine.

8.Pesarattu

A green gram (moong dal) crepe that is typically served with ginger chutney or coconut chutney. It’s a nutritious and tasty breakfast option.
