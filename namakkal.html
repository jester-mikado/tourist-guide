Namakkal

Namakkal is a district located in the southern part of Tamil Nadu, India. Known for its rich cultural heritage and historical significance, Namakkal is often referred to as the "Granary of Tamil Nadu" due to its agricultural productivity. The district is famous for its temples, particularly the Namakkal Narasimha Perumal Temple, and its vibrant community festivals.

Narasimha Perumal Temple

An important Vaishnavite temple dedicated to Lord Narasimha, an incarnation of Vishnu. It is renowned for its architectural beauty and religious significance.

TIMING: 6:00 AM to 12:00 PM and from 4:00 PM to 8:00 PM

Anjaneyar Temple

This temple is dedicated to Lord Hanuman and is known for its large statue of Hanuman. It attracts many devotees and tourists for its spiritual ambiance.

TIMING: 6:00 AM to 12:00 PM and from 4:00 PM to 8:00 PM.

Tiruchengode

Famous for the Tiruchengode Arulmigu Neminath Jain Temple and the hilltop temple of Lord Arthanareeswarar. The area is also known for its scenic beauty and religious significance.

TIMING::6:00 AM to 12:00 PM and from 4:00 PM to 8:00 PM

Kolli Hills

A scenic hill station known for its lush green landscapes, numerous waterfalls, and trekking opportunities. It’s a great spot for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts.

TIMING : 24/7
Best time :During daylight hours for safety and to fully enjoy the scenic beauty.

Sendamangalam

Known for its serene environment and historical temples. It's a quieter destination, ideal for those looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life.

TIMING:Generally open during daylight hours; specific temple timings may vary

Rasipuram

Known for the famous Rasipuram Sri Kalyana Perumal Temple and its rich history. It’s a town with a blend of spiritual and cultural attractions.

TIMING : 6:00 AM to 12:00 PM and from 4:00 PM to 8:00 PM.

Sengattupatti

Known for its ancient temples and historical monuments, this village offers a glimpse into traditional Tamil Nadu architecture and culture.

Timing :6:00 AM to 12:00 PM and 4:00 PM to 8:00 PM.

Kumarapalayam

This area is known for its beautiful temples and serene rural landscapes. The Kumarapalayam Murugan Temple is particularly significant, attracting devotees from nearby regions.

TIMING:6:00 AM to 12:00 PM and from 4:00 PM to 8:00 PM.

Pachamalai Hills

Often referred to as the "Poor Man's Ooty," the Pachamalai Hills offer picturesque views, lush greenery, and a peaceful atmosphere. It's a great spot for trekking and enjoying nature.

TIMING: 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM






Foods To Try


1. Chicken Curry

A local specialty, this spicy and flavorful chicken curry is known for its rich taste and aromatic spices. It often includes a blend of local ingredients and is a staple in the region's cuisine.

2.Kollimalai Keerai

Kollimalai Keerai (also known as "Kolli Hills Greens") is a leafy vegetable that grows in the Kolli Hills area of Namakkal. It’s often used in traditional dishes like sambar and poriyal, known for its unique taste and nutritional benefits.

3.Namakkal Puttu

A type of steamed rice cake, Namakkal Puttu is made with rice flour and grated coconut. It’s usually served with a variety of side dishes such as curries or banana.

4.Kollihills Coffee

Coffee from the Kolli Hills region of Namakkal is known for its distinct aroma and flavor. The cool climate of the hills contributes to the unique taste of the coffee grown there.

5.Sundakkai Curry

Sundakkai, or Turkey Berry, is used in various South Indian dishes. In Namakkal, it's often prepared as a tangy curry or as part of a traditional sambar, reflecting the local taste preferences.

6.Idiyappam with Curry

Idiyappam, also known as string hoppers, is a steamed dish made from rice flour, typically served with various types of curries, such as coconut milk-based or spicy meat curries.

7.Rava Kesari

This is a traditional South Indian sweet made from semolina (rava), sugar, ghee, and flavored with cardamom and saffron. In Namakkal, it is often made with a distinctive texture and sweetness that reflects local preferences.

8.Vatha Kuzhambu

A spicy and tangy tamarind-based curry, Vatha Kuzhambu includes dried vegetables or vathals (like sundakkai or manathakkali) and is commonly served with rice. This dish is a staple in Namakkal households, showcasing the region's love for bold, spicy flavors.
