Chennai

Chennai, the vibrant capital of Tamil Nadu, is a city where tradition and modernity blend seamlessly. Nestled on the Coromandel Coast of the Bay of Bengal, it's known for its rich cultural heritage, bustling Marina Beach, and historic temples. With a thriving arts scene, delectable South Indian cuisine, and a dynamic economy, Chennai is a melting pot of experiences that reflect both its deep-rooted history and its progressive spirit.

Fort St. George

This historic fort, built by the British East India Company in 1644, now houses the Tamil Nadu Legislative Assembly and a museum with artifacts from the colonial period.

TIMING:9:00 AM to 5:00 PM
Best Month: November to February

Marina Beach

One of the longest urban beaches in the world, Marina Beach is a popular spot for both locals and tourists. Enjoy the sunset, stroll along the shore, and try some local street food.

BEST TIME TO VISIT
Early Morning(4:30 to 6:00 AM)
Evening(5:00 to 7:00PM)

Kapaleeshwarar Temple

Kapaleeshwarar Temple in Chennai's Mylapore is a revered Hindu site dedicated to Lord Shiva, renowned for its stunning Dravidian architecture and intricately carved gopuram. Established in ancient times, it remains a vibrant spiritual and cultural hub. The annual Panguni Perumal festival highlights its rich traditions and communal spirit.

BEST TIME TO VISIT
Morning ( 6:00 AM to 8:00 AM)
Event:(Panguni Perumal festival) in March-April

The Government Museum

The Government Museum in Chennai, established in 1851, is one of the oldest museums in India, showcasing a vast collection of art, archaeology, and numismatics. It features notable exhibits like the Bronze Gallery and the Art Gallery.

TIMING :9:30 AM to 5:00 PM
CLose: Mondays and public holidays.

Santhome Basilica

Santhome Basilica in Chennai is a prominent Roman Catholic church built over the tomb of St. Thomas the Apostle, who is believed to have been martyred there. The basilica is renowned for its neo-gothic architecture and historical significance.

TIMING:Morning ( 6:00 AM to 8:00 AM)
Evening:6:00PM

Vivekananda House

Vivekananda House in Chennai, also known as Vivekananda Illam, is a historic building where Swami Vivekananda stayed in 1897 during his visit to the city. The house has been converted into a museum dedicated to his life and teachings, showcasing various artifacts and exhibits related to his philosophy

TIMING : 9:30 AM to 6:00 PM
Close:Monday

Birla Planetarium

The Birla Planetarium in Chennai offers an immersive experience into the cosmos with its state-of-the-art digital planetarium shows and educational exhibits. It features a range of programs on astronomy and space science, aimed at both children and adults.

Timing :10:00 AM to 5:30 PM
Close:Mondays and public holidays.

DakshinaChitra

DakshinaChitra, located near Chennai, is a cultural heritage museum showcasing the traditional art, crafts, and lifestyle of South India. It features authentic homes, workshops, and exhibits from various southern states, providing a unique insight into regional traditions and craftsmanship.

TIMING: 10:00 AM to 6:00 PM
Close:Mondays and public holidays.

Ekkatuthangal Park

Ekkatuthangal Park, located in the Ekkatuthangal area of Chennai, is a well-maintained green space offering a serene environment for relaxation and recreational activities. It features walking paths, children's play areas, and lush landscaping.

TIMING: 6:00 AM to 9:00 AM and 4:00 PM to 7:00 PM

Theosophical Society

The Theosophical Society in Chennai, established in 1882, is a serene and historic campus dedicated to spiritual and philosophical study. The society's grounds feature beautiful gardens, an old banyan tree, and the notable Adyar Library and Research Centre.

TIMING : 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM
Close:Sundays.






Foods To Try


1.Idli and Sambar

Soft, steamed rice cakes (idlis) paired with a flavorful lentil stew (sambar). It's a staple breakfast dish in Chennai and often served with coconut chutney.

2.Sundal

A light and healthy snack made from chickpeas or other legumes, sautéed with mustard seeds, curry leaves, and grated coconut.

3.Mutton Chukka

A spicy, dry-fried mutton dish that is rich in flavor and typically enjoyed as an accompaniment to rice or as a standalone snack.

4.Kothu Parotta

A popular street food made by chopping up flaky parottas (layered flatbreads) and stir-frying them with a mix of vegetables, meat, and spices.

5.Chettinad Chicken

A spicy, aromatic chicken curry from the Chettinad region, known for its bold flavors and complex spice blends.

6.Vada

Savory fritters made from urad dal (black gram), often served with coconut chutney and sambar.

7.Biriyani

Chennai-style biriyani, often made with aromatic basmati rice, tender meat (like chicken or mutton), and a blend of spices. It’s often accompanied by raita (yogurt sauce) and salad.

8.Filter Coffee

Strong, aromatic coffee made using a traditional South Indian filter. It’s typically served with hot milk and sugar.
