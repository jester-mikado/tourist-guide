Kanyakumari

Kanyakumari, situated at the southern tip of India, is a coastal town renowned for its breathtaking views where the Arabian Sea, the Bay of Bengal, and the Indian Ocean converge. Famous for its stunning sunrise and sunset vistas, it is also home to the iconic Vivekananda Rock Memorial and the 133-foot-tall Thiruvalluvar Statue.

Vivekananda Rock Memorial

Vivekananda Rock Memorial: A prominent monument dedicated to Swami Vivekananda, located on a rocky island, offering panoramic views of the ocean.

TIMING:7:00 AM to 4:00 PM and 5:00 PM to 6:00 PM
Close: Tuesday

Thiruvalluvar Statue

An imposing 133-foot statue of the Tamil poet and philosopher Thiruvalluvar, situated on a separate rock island.

TIMING: 7:00 AM to 4:00 PM and 5:00 PM to 6:00 PM

Kanyakumari Temple

A historic Hindu temple dedicated to the goddess Kanyakumari, revered for its architectural beauty and spiritual significance.

TIMING:: 4:00 AM to 12:00 PM and 4:00 PM to 8:00 PM

Sunrise and Sunset Points

The dramatic views of the sunrise and sunset over the ocean from various points in Kanyakumari are spectacular and not to be missed.

TIMING : 24/7
Best time : Early Morning

Gandhi Memorial

This memorial is built on the spot where Mahatma Gandhi's ashes were kept before being immersed in the sea, featuring a unique architectural design aligned with the sun's rays.

TIMING:7:00 AM to 7:00 PM

Padmanabhapuram Palace

Located a bit outside Kanyakumari, this well-preserved palace is a fine example of traditional Kerala architecture and royal heritage.

TIMING : 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM
Close:Sunday and public Holidays

Cape Comorin

The southernmost tip of the Indian subcontinent, offering stunning views and a unique sense of place where three seas meet.

Timing : Full Day

Sankara Pillai’s House

A historical house that offers insight into the traditional lifestyle of the region and showcases local artifacts.

TIMING: 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM

Vattakottai Fort

An old coastal fort offering a glimpse into the area's military history and scenic views of the surrounding landscape.

TIMING: 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM

Nagercoil

A nearby town known for its temples and vibrant markets, adding a cultural dimension to your visit.

TIMING :9:00 AM to 6:00 PM
Close:Sundays and Saturday






Foods To Try


1.Fish Curry

A spicy and tangy curry made with fresh fish, often featuring a unique blend of local spices and coconut.

2. Appam

A type of rice pancake with a soft, spongy center and crispy edges, often served with coconut milk or stew.

3.Banana Chips

Though not exclusive to Kanyakumari, the banana chips here are often made from local varieties of bananas and are a popular snack.

4.Seafood Specialties

Given its coastal location, Kanyakumari offers a variety of fresh seafood dishes, including crab, prawns, and squid, cooked in local styles.

5.Kunnu (Sweet) Rice

A traditional rice dish that might be prepared with jaggery and coconut, offering a sweet and savory flavor.

6.Idiyappam with Curry

Idiyappam, also known as string hoppers, is a steamed dish made from rice flour, typically served with various types of curries, such as coconut milk-based or spicy meat curries.

7.Biriyani

Chennai-style biriyani, often made with aromatic basmati rice, tender meat (like chicken or mutton), and a blend of spices. It’s often accompanied by raita (yogurt sauce) and salad.

8. Coconut Milk Rice

This dish features rice cooked with coconut milk, creating a creamy and subtly sweet flavor. It’s often seasoned with spices and sometimes served with vegetables or fish.
