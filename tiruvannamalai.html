Tiruvannamalai

Tiruvannamalai, located in the southern state of Tamil Nadu, India, is a district known for its rich cultural and spiritual heritage. The district is famously home to the sacred Arunachala Hill, a major pilgrimage site associated with the revered saint Ramana Maharshi. Tiruvannamalai's landscape features a blend of ancient temples, lush greenery, and traditional villages

Arunachala Hill

A sacred mountain and spiritual center, often associated with the revered sage Ramana Maharshi. The hill is considered a manifestation of Lord Shiva.

TIMING:24/7
Best visited early in the morning or late in the afternoon

Sri Ramana Maharshi Ashram

The ashram of Ramana Maharshi, a renowned spiritual teacher. It includes his meditation hall and living quarters, offering a peaceful environment for contemplation.

TIMING: 6:00 AM to 12:00 PM and 2:00 PM to 6:00 PM.

Sri Arunachaleswarar Temple

A major Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, located at the base of Arunachala Hill. It is known for its grand architecture and spiritual significance.

TIMING:: 5:00 AM to 12:30 PM and 4:00 PM to 9:00 PM.

Virupaksha Cave

One of the many caves on Arunachala Hill where Ramana Maharshi meditated. It offers a serene setting and a glimpse into the sage’s life.

TIMING : Generally open from early morning until evening

Skandashramam

Another cave on Arunachala Hill where Ramana Maharshi spent time. It’s located further up the hill and is more secluded.

TIMING: Accessible during daylight hours

Seshadri Swamigal Ashram

Dedicated to Seshadri Swamigal, a contemporary of Ramana Maharshi known for his spiritual teachings. The ashram is a place for meditation and reflection.

TIMING : 6:00 AM to 12:00 PM and 4:00 PM to 8:00 PM.

Tiruvannamalai Fort

A historical fort built by the Vijayanagar kings. It offers a glimpse into the region’s history and panoramic views of the surrounding area.

Timing : 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM.

Chengam

Known for its scenic beauty and the Chengam Lake, which is a serene spot for relaxation and picnics.

TIMING: 24/7






Foods To Try


1.Tiruvannamalai Pori

A type of puffed rice snack, often enjoyed as a light, crispy treat. It’s usually seasoned with spices and sometimes mixed with nuts and curry leaves.

2.Vella Kesari

A sweet dish made from semolina (rava), ghee, and sugar. It’s a popular dessert in South Indian cuisine and often served during special occasions.

3.Mutton Chukka

A spicy, dry-fried mutton dish that is well-seasoned with local spices. It’s known for its rich, robust flavor and is a favorite among meat lovers.

4.Idiyappam with Mutton Curry

Idiyappam, or string hoppers, are steamed rice noodles that are often served with a spicy mutton curry. The combination is a regional favorite.

5.Kollu Sundal

Made from horse gram (kollu), this dish is usually prepared with spices, coconut, and curry leaves. It’s a nutritious and flavorful snack.

6.Sambar

While Sambar is common throughout Tamil Nadu, the version found in Tiruvannamalai might have unique local twists in terms of spices and ingredients.

7.Ragi Mudde

This is a traditional dish made from finger millet flour, cooked into a thick, dough-like consistency. It’s often served with spicy vegetable stews or sambar.

8.Kadalai Paruppu Sundal

A type of sundal made from chickpeas (kadalai paruppu), seasoned with grated coconut, curry leaves, and spices. It’s a popular snack or side dish, especially during festivals.
